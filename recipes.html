1 Lechon Kawali
Ingredients:
  • 2 lbs. pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons whole pepper corn
  • 5 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 3 cups cooking Oil
  • 34 ounces Water
Instructions:
  1. Pour water on cooking pot and put it to a boil.
  2. Add the pork belly and 1 tbsp salt. Put some pepper and bay leaves. Let it boil for around 30 mins. or until the meat gets tender
  3. Remove the meat from the pot and let it cool down for a few minutes
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of salt on the pork belly. Distribute it evenly on all sections.
  5. Heat oil on a cooking pot and fry the meat.
  6. Fry until crispy, then turn the meat over to completely fry the opposite side until crispy. Note: Be extra careful when frying, as oil can splatter. You can use the pot's cover to cover it while oil splatters, but do not cover it completely, as steam will be trapped, causing more splatters.
  7. Remove the meat from the cooking pot. Arrange on a wire rack to cool down. Slice according to desired portions. Serve with sauce.

2 Calumpit Longganisa
Ingredients:
  • 1 ¾ lbs. ground pork
  • 9 tablespoons dark brown suga
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 ¼ tablespoons coarse salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 head garlic
Instructions:
  1. Remove the garlic cloves' outer layer. Using a mortar and pestle, crush completely. Crushed garlic should be minced. Put aside.
  2. Put the ground pork and all the ingredients in a large mixing bowl. Stir thoroughly.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate it for 2 hours.
  4. Lay a piece of wax paper on a level surface and shape the longganisa. Cover the wax paper with about 3 teaspoons of the mixture. Cover the mixture by folding the wax paper from top to bottom. Slide the card in the direction of the mixture while holding the paper's edge with your fingers. Continue pushing until a sausage-like shape is achieved. Continue doing this until all of the concoction has been drunk.
  5. In a skillet, combine 1 cup water and 4 teaspoons cooking oil to cook the longganisa.
  6. After adding the longganisa, turn on the heat. Turn the heat down to the lowest level after letting the liquid boil. Keep boiling until all of the water has evaporated.
  7. Fry both sides of the longganisa in the remaining oil until it turns brown.
  8. Remove from the pan and serve with rice.

3 Kare-Kare Malolos
Ingredients:
  • 3 lbs oxtail cut in 2 inch slices
  • 1 piece small banana flower bud sliced
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy
  • 1 bundle string beans cut into 2 inch slices
  • 4 pieces eggplants sliced
  • 1 cup ground peanuts
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup shrimp paste
  • 34 Ounces water
  • 1/2 cup annatto seeds soaked in a cup of water
  • 1/2 cup toasted ground rice
  • 1 tbsp of minced garlic
  • 1 piece onion
  • salt and pepper
Instructions:
  1. Boil the water in a large pot.
  2. Put the oxtail and onions and let it simmer for 2 to 3 hours.
  3. Once tender, add ground peanuts, peanut butter, and the water from the annatto seed mixture. Let it simmer for 5 to 7 minutes.
  4. Add the toasted ground rice and let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  5. On a different pan, saute the garlic, banana flower, eggplant, and string beans.
  6. Add the cooked vegetables to the large pot.
  7. Add salt and pepper.
  8. Serve with hot shrimp paste and enjoy.

4 Pastillas de Leche
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups of powdered milk
  • 14 oz condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
Instructions:
  1. In a large mixing bowl, add the condensed milk.
  2. Slowly fold-in the powdered milk.
  3. Mold them into cylinders or balls and coat them in sugar.
  4. Wrap in colorful paper/cellophane. Serve and enjoy!
